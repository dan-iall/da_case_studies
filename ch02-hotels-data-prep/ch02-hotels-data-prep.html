üïµÔ∏è‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è Bellonda‚Äôs Logic Decoder

The Business Goal: Detect data quality issues by isolating hotel entities that appear multiple times in our dataset. We must view all instances to determine if they are redundant duplicates or conflicting data points.

The Syntax Anatomy: * sort_values(by=...) ‚Üí The Prep: Groups identical IDs together visually for easier human inspection. * duplicated(keep=False) ‚Üí The Dragnet: The critical setting. Instead of hiding the original, it marks ALL occurrences (original + copies) as True so we can compare them side-by-side. * [[list_of_cols]] ‚Üí The Lens: Restricts our view to only the ‚Äúvital signs‚Äù (price, stars) needed to make a decision on which record to keep.

üí° The Wisdom Check: Never use generic methods blindly. Default duplicated() hides the problem (the original row). Using keep=False is the only way to perform a true audit of the conflict.

üïµÔ∏è‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è Bellonda‚Äôs Logic Decoder

The Business Goal: Check for Systematic Bias. We suspect missing ratings aren‚Äôt random accidents. This table reveals if specific business segments (like ‚ÄòApartments‚Äô) are disproportionately responsible for the missing data.

The Syntax Anatomy: * pd.crosstab(row, col) ‚Üí The Matrix: Counts the intersection of two categorical variables. * row (Accom Type) vs col (Misrating) ‚Üí The X-Ray: Breaks down our missing data flag by business category. * margins=True ‚Üí The Context: Adds ‚ÄúAll‚Äù (Subtotals). Without this, we can‚Äôt calculate percentages or judge the severity of the count.

üí° The Wisdom Check: Absolute numbers lie. ‚Äú100 missing values‚Äù sounds the same as ‚Äú100 missing values.‚Äù But 100 out of 200 (50%) vs. 100 out of 1,000,000 (0.01%) are completely different business problems. Always use margins=True.

üïµÔ∏è‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è Bellonda‚Äôs Logic Decoder

The Business Goal: value-based Impact Analysis. We know where data is missing (frequency), now we check if it affects Revenue (Price). We are comparing the average price of labeled vs. unlabeled data to check for ‚ÄúSelection Bias‚Äù (e.g., ‚ÄúCheaper hotels have more missing data‚Äù).

The Syntax Anatomy: * values= + aggfunc= ‚Üí The Pivot: These must always appear together. * values: The column containing the numbers we want to crunch (Price). * aggfunc: The math operation to apply (Mean/Average). * margins=True with mean ‚Üí The Weighted Average: Be careful! The ‚ÄúAll‚Äù row here isn‚Äôt a sum; it is the average price of the entire category.

üí° The Wisdom Check: Defines the difference between a ‚ÄúData Janitor‚Äù and a ‚ÄúData Scientist.‚Äù The Janitor counts the missing rows. The Scientist calculates if the missing rows represent a specific economic segment (Budget vs. Luxury).

pd.crosstab(
